What are the elements of arson?

Arson sits under s267 of the Crimes Act 1961. To prove it, you need three things:

  • The defendant intentionally or recklessly set fire to, or destroyed, property by fire
  • The property was a building, structure, or part of one
  • The act caused damage to that property

The mens rea element is important — both intention and recklessness will satisfy the standard, so you don't need to prove they meant to cause the specific extent of damage, just that they knew fire was likely.

What does recklessness mean here?

Recklessness in this context means the defendant was aware of the risk that their actions could cause a fire, but went ahead anyway — consciously disregarding that risk.
